Lesser-Known Health Metrics & Their Importance

Health Metric What It Measures Why It Matters How to Improve It
HRV (Heart Rate Variability) Variation in time between heartbeats Higher HRV indicates better stress resilience & cardiovascular health Regular exercise, deep breathing, quality sleep
Glycemic Variability Fluctuations in blood sugar levels throughout the day Lower variability reduces diabetes risk & improves energy levels Eat balanced meals, avoid processed sugars
Grip Strength Muscle function & overall longevity Strong grip correlates with lower risk of heart disease & mortality Strength training, hand exercises
Sleep Chronotype Your body's natural sleep-wake cycle Aligning lifestyle with chronotype improves energy & cognitive function Adjust bedtime & routine based on early bird/night owl tendencies
TMAO (Trimethylamine N-oxide) Gut microbiome marker linked to heart disease High levels increase risk of atherosclerosis Eat more fiber, reduce red meat consumption
VO2 Max Maximum oxygen uptake during exercise Higher VO2 Max is linked to better cardiovascular health & endurance Interval training, regular aerobic exercise
Mitochondrial Function Efficiency of cellular energy production Declining function leads to fatigue & aging Fasting, cold exposure, antioxidants
ALT & AST Levels Liver enzyme levels indicating liver health Elevated levels suggest liver stress or disease Reduce alcohol, eat anti-inflammatory foods
